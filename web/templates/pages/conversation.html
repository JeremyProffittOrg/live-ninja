Troubleshooting tips
  • Flat line / no level? Check the mic's physical mute switch and that it's plugged in, then speak at normal volume about an arm's length away.
  • Chrome blocked the mic: click the tune (or lock) icon at the left of the address bar → Site settings → set Microphone to Allow, then reload this page. You can also open chrome://settings/content/microphone and remove live.jeremy.ninja from the "Not allowed" list.
  • Wrong microphone? Chrome records from your system default mic unless you pick one on the Settings page. On Windows, change the default under Settings → System → Sound → Input.
  • Windows is blocking desktop apps: Windows Settings → Privacy & security → Microphone — turn on microphone access and "Let desktop apps access your microphone".
  • Another app is holding the mic (shows as "could not open the microphone"): close Zoom, Teams, OBS, or other recording apps and test again.
  • Level moves here but the assistant doesn't respond: raise the input volume in Windows Sound settings, and make sure you speak after the status pill says Listening.
